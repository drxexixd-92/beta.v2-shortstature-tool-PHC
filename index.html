⚠ Important: Clinical Assessment Required Before Using This Tool

This growth chart tool is a clinical aid — not a standalone diagnostic instrument. Before plotting measurements, ensure you have completed:

📋 Comprehensive HistoryBirth history (IUGR, prematurity), medications (steroids, ADHD, anticonvulsants), family history of short stature or delayed puberty, nutritional history, chronic illness, parental heights
🔍 Physical ExaminationBody proportions (upper/lower segment ratio), dysmorphic features, signs of Turner syndrome (webbed neck, lymphedema), midline abnormalities, goiter, nutritional status
📈 Serial Growth AssessmentPlot at least 2 measurements over time to calculate growth velocity. A single point is insufficient for diagnosis — pattern and trend matter
🪨 Bone Age (if indicated)Left hand/wrist X-ray is a key diagnostic step when short stature is confirmed. Bone age guides IGF-1 interpretation (vs bone age, not chronological age)
Definition: Short stature = height ≤2.3rd percentile (<-2 SD below the mean for age and sex). >60% of children with short stature have non-pathological variants. Pathological causes account for ~5%.
